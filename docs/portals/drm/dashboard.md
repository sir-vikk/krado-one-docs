# Incident Dashboard

The Incident Dashboard is the landing page of the DRM (Disaster Recovery & Maintenance) Portal. It provides a real-time snapshot of your organization's incident landscape, helping you quickly assess operational health and identify areas that need attention.

## What You See

When you open the dashboard, four key areas give you an at-a-glance summary of your incident posture.

### Status Cards

Four summary cards across the top of the page display incident counts by lifecycle stage:

| Card | What It Tells You |
|------|-------------------|
| **Open** | Incidents that have been acknowledged but are not yet resolved |
| **Investigating** | Incidents currently being diagnosed by your team |
| **Resolved** | Incidents where the root cause has been identified and a fix applied |
| **Closed** | Incidents with a completed post-mortem and filed RCA |

### Severity Distribution Chart

A horizontal bar chart breaks down your incidents by severity level -- **Critical**, **High**, **Medium**, and **Low**. Use this chart to spot concentration risk and determine whether high-severity incidents are trending up or down.

### Key Metrics

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| Total Incidents | All incidents recorded across the lifetime of your account |
| RCA Completed | Number of incidents with a filed root cause analysis |
| Preventive Actions Done | Action items that have been marked complete |
| Avg Resolution Time | Mean time to resolution across all closed incidents |

### Recent Incidents

The bottom panel lists the most recent incidents, showing severity, status, and affected products for each. Click any row to navigate directly to the full incident detail view.

## How to Navigate from the Dashboard

1. Open the **DRM Portal** from the left sidebar.
2. The dashboard loads as the default view.
3. Click any status card to filter the incidents list by that status.
4. Click a row in the recent incidents table to open the incident detail page.
5. Use the left sidebar links to navigate to other DRM sections such as **Incidents**, **Maintenance**, or **Reports**.
